Clinical significance
- The combination of unexpected THC and amphetamine positivity plus absence of prescribed opioid on screening is concerning for aberrant drug test results and possible nonadherence, undisclosed substance use, diversion, or substance use disorder.

  - Clarify the opioid result with a definitive opioid panel that includes hydrocodone and hydromorphone (and, if available, norhydrocodone).

Management Plan

Immediate steps
1. Review the controlled-substance agreement and document the discussion.
2. Discuss the UDS results nonjudgmentally with the patient:
   - confirm timing of last hydrocodone dose,
   - ask about OTC products, supplements, weight-loss/energy agents, or borrowed medications that could explain amphetamine positivity,
   - assess cannabis, stimulant, and other substance use.
3. Check prescription monitoring program (if not already done) and reconcile all controlled substances from outside sources.
4. Consider pill count, shorter refill interval, and repeat random UDS if opioid prescribing is continued at all.

Pain management decision algorithm
- If confirmatory testing is negative for amphetamines/THC and hydrocodone is present at expected levels:
  - reassess pain diagnosis, function, and opioid benefit vs risk,
  - consider a limited/structured opioid plan with close monitoring, or taper if benefits do not clearly outweigh risks.
- If confirmatory testing confirms nonprescribed substances and/or hydrocodone remains absent:
  - treat as a significant opioid safety event,
  - do not continue routine opioid prescribing,
  - avoid escalation,
  - implement a taper/stop plan if physiologically appropriate and safe.
- If there are signs of opioid use disorder or inability to adhere to prescribed therapy:
  - refer/transition to addiction medicine,
  - consider medication-assisted treatment with buprenorphine/naloxone.

Opioid use disorder considerations
- The documented diagnosis of opioid dependence and aberrant UDS raise concern for opioid use disorder or unsafe opioid use.
- Addiction medicine evaluation is appropriate to determine candidacy for buprenorphine/naloxone.
- If the patient is in withdrawal or has clear OUD, buprenorphine-based treatment is preferred over ongoing full-agonist opioid therapy.

Specialty referral
- Pain management referral: warranted for non-opioid multimodal pain strategies and reassessment of chronic pain treatment.
- Chemical dependency/addiction medicine: warranted to evaluate for OUD and consider buprenorphine/naloxone transition.
- Consider behavioral health support if substance-use counseling is available.

Recommended follow-up

- Close follow-up within days to review confirmatory results, PDMP findings, and referral status.
