Interpretation of Findings
1) Unexpected negative: oxycodone not detected despite active oxycodone ER prescription.
- Clinical significance: Concerning for nonadherence, intermittent use with recent missed doses, running out early, diversion, or assay/timing issues.
- Opiates negative is not necessarily abnormal because standard opiate immunoassays often do not reliably detect oxycodone; however, the oxycodone-specific negative is clinically important.

2) Unexpected positive: benzodiazepines positive.
- Clinical significance: Unexpected because no benzodiazepine is prescribed. Patient reported exposure to an unspecified "sleeping pill" from a family member, raising concern for non-prescribed sedative use and additive overdose risk with opioids.
- Requires confirmatory testing to identify the specific agent and assess for false-positive immunoassay results.

3) Cannabinoids positive.
- Clinical significance: Consistent with documented daily marijuana use; not unexpected based on history, but relevant to counseling and overall safety planning.

4) Other substances negative.

Clinical Significance in Context
- The active problem list includes opioid dependence (F11.20), which increases concern for opioid-related risk and warrants a structured reassessment for opioid use disorder (OUD) versus physiologic dependence.
- An absent prescribed opioid plus unexpected benzodiazepine exposure is a clinically meaningful safety signal and may indicate nonadherence, diversion, or substance misuse.
- Daily marijuana use should be discussed as part of the overall controlled-substance risk assessment.

Clinical Decision Algorithm / Next Steps
1) Immediate safety and verification
- Review timing of last oxycodone dose, nausea/vomiting, missed doses, and any potential urine dilution.
- Reconcile all medications, including any outside prescriptions and over-the-counter sleep aids.
- Check PDMP again for undisclosed controlled substances.
- Await confirmatory testing before irreversible decisions when clinically safe to do so.

2) Counseling now
- Advise patient to stop taking any non-prescribed sedatives or "sleeping pills."
- Counsel on high overdose risk when opioids are combined with benzodiazepines or other sedatives.
- Provide/renew naloxone and overdose education if not already in place.

3) Opioid prescribing while pending confirmation
- Do not abruptly discontinue opioid therapy based solely on a single immunoassay result.
- Increase monitoring intensity: shorter refill interval, close follow-up, and consideration of random UDS and pill counts.
- Reassess whether the current opioid regimen remains appropriate given the concerning result pattern and the problem-list history of opioid dependence.

4) If confirmatory testing shows oxycodone absent and benzodiazepine confirmed
- Reassess adherence, diversion risk, and the overall risk-benefit balance of opioid therapy.
- Perform a focused assessment for OUD using DSM-5 criteria (loss of control, craving, continued use despite harm, inability to adhere to treatment, etc.).
- If OUD is suspected or confirmed, discuss medication treatment options, especially buprenorphine, and refer to addiction medicine.
- If opioid therapy continues, use tighter monitoring: frequent visits, random toxicology testing, pill counts, and a renewed treatment agreement.

5) If confirmatory testing shows a false-positive benzodiazepine result and oxycodone is present
- Continue opioid therapy only if overall risk remains acceptable.
- Reinforce adherence, safe storage, avoidance of non-prescribed medications, and adherence to the controlled-substance agreement.
- Maintain or increase monitoring depending on clinical judgment.

6) Specialty referral triggers
- Addiction medicine referral if confirmatory testing or clinical assessment suggests OUD, recurrent non-prescribed sedative use, or loss of control over controlled substances.
- Pain management referral if pain control is inadequate, opioid risk is high, or opioid-sparing strategies are needed.
- Behavioral health referral if insomnia/anxiety is driving use of non-prescribed sedatives.
