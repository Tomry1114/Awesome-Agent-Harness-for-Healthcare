Indicated hormonal workup and rationale

1) Autonomous cortisol secretion screening
- Indicated for all adrenal incidentalomas regardless of size.
- Ordered/active plan: 1 mg overnight dexamethasone suppression test with 8 AM serum cortisol.
- Preparation/instructions:
  - Take dexamethasone 1 mg by mouth once at 11:00 PM.
  - Have blood drawn for serum cortisol at approximately 8:00 AM the next morning.
  - If cortisol is not suppressed, confirm timing/adherence and consider checking ACTH and DHEA-S and repeating/confirming testing before labeling autonomous cortisol secretion.
- Interpretation framework:
  - Cortisol suppression to <=1.8 mcg/dL is generally reassuring.
  - Post-DST cortisol >1.8 mcg/dL suggests possible mild autonomous cortisol secretion, especially relevant here due to prediabetes and hypertension; if abnormal, evaluate ACTH-independence and cardiometabolic comorbidities and consider endocrine surgery referral depending on degree of cortisol autonomy, imaging phenotype, and comorbidity burden.

2) Pheochromocytoma screening
- Indicated because lesion is not fully characterized by dedicated adrenal CT/HU in the available report, and patient has palpitations/SVT history. If a lesion is unequivocally lipid-rich homogeneous adenoma with unenhanced HU <=10, some guidelines allow omission of metanephrine testing; those imaging criteria are not documented here.
- Ordered/active plan: plasma free metanephrines.
- Preparation/instructions:
  - Avoid caffeine, nicotine, and vigorous exercise before the draw if feasible.
  - Draw ideally after resting supine for 20-30 minutes; if supine collection is unavailable, document seated collection because it increases false-positive rates.
  - Review interfering medications/supplements if results are borderline.
- Contingency:
  - If plasma metanephrines are clearly elevated, avoid adrenal biopsy or surgery until pheochromocytoma is addressed; confirm with repeat optimized testing or 24-hour urine fractionated metanephrines as appropriate, then proceed with alpha-blockade and localization/planning.
  - If borderline elevation, repeat under optimal supine rested conditions and assess for physiologic/medication causes.

3) Primary aldosteronism screening
- Indicated because adrenal incidentaloma plus treated hypertension is present. Normal potassium does not exclude primary aldosteronism.
- Ordered/active plan: plasma aldosterone concentration and plasma renin activity/concentration, with simultaneous basic metabolic panel/potassium.
- Preparation/instructions:
  - Morning draw preferred.
  - Patient should maintain usual sodium intake.
  - Correct hypokalemia before testing if present; current potassium is normal.
  - Ideally draw after patient has been up for at least 2 hours and seated for 5-15 minutes, if feasible.
  - Continue current antihypertensives unless directed; document that irbesartan and amlodipine may affect interpretation, particularly irbesartan increasing renin and potentially lowering ARR.
- Contingency:
  - If ARR is positive with inappropriately elevated aldosterone and suppressed renin, confirm primary aldosteronism per protocol unless phenotype is unequivocal. Then consider adrenal vein sampling if surgical therapy is contemplated, given patient age and need to distinguish unilateral adenoma from bilateral disease.
  - If ARR is negative but clinical suspicion remains high, repeat after medication adjustment to less-interfering agents if safe.
